    Biological Collections in Brazil

    What are biological collections?

    Biological collections are groups of organisms organized to provide information about origin, collection and identification of each of its specimens.

    They are divided into four categories:

    • Microbiological collections
    • Zoological collections
    • Histopathological collection
    • Botanical Collection

    The specimens reveal the genetic diversity of all living groups. The botanical specimens receive the name of exsiccates - dried and pressed samples.

    The individuals preserved constitute the epidemiological memory and record of variations occurring in etiological agents over time. They represent the genetic populations of organisms related to public health research and microbiological collections with various potentialities for biotechnology. As sources of genetic resources, the biological collections offer qualified products and services for application in Research, Development and Innovation, which include, for example, the production of inputs for vaccines and medicines.

    Currently, Brazilian Universities and researchers are working to informatize their collections and provide information online.
